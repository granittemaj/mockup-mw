01 · The DiscoveryTwenty years across six continents and 33 countries

He helped stop the trafficking of children in a region of the Peruvian Amazon, but not in the way you might think. He used a beverage company, built alongside indigenous communities, that made Brazil nuts worth more than forced labor ever could. He helped replicate that model in Amsterdam, where a restaurant in the city's Red Light District became one of the most talked-about social enterprises in Europe.

None of it was built alone. These are stories of communities, collaborators, using diverse capital sources, and a stubborn refusal to accept that the way things are is the way they have to stay. Build something that replaces the problem, and the rest follows.

His talks don't make you feel inspired for a day. They give you a framework for building something that lasts.

01
Out-createv. /aʊt·kriˈeɪt/
To fight injustice not by opposing it, but by building something better that makes it obsolete.
02
Pragmatic Idealismn. the daily practice
Dreaming at the scale of the actual problem, and acting with the discipline the problem demands.
03
Ruthless Executionn. the discipline
Choosing the direction before accelerating the speed, and then refusing to stop.
04
Aggressive Humilityn. the hardest courage
Acting on the evidence that what you built is not working, before sunk cost makes the truth impossible to face.
05
Impact Stackn. three questions
The real root cause, not the symptom. What you actually have, not what you wish. What the problem demands, not what is easy to fund.
06
Freedom of Constraintn. the observation
Limited resources force the most creative solutions, by eliminating every option except the one the problem requires.
07
The Montara Circlen. a method
A two-day convening that produces a committed outcome. The first produced REBBL. The second, Dignita.
